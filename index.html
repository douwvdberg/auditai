The Problem

Everyone's using AI.
Nobody's checking the output.

AI tools generate content fast. The problem isn't speed — it's that nobody built a quality gate for what comes out the other side.

📋

Vendor deliverables

Your agency sends AI-drafted reports. Your contractor submits AI-written code comments. How do you know it's actually good — not just grammatically correct?

👥

Hiring & applications

Job applicants use AI for cover letters and work samples. Managers have no systematic way to distinguish fluent-sounding from genuinely capable.

📣

Internal content

Marketing, legal, and comms teams ship AI-generated material without a quality rubric. "Looks fine" isn't a standard. Brand damage and legal liability are real.

🔍

Detection is the wrong question

Existing tools ask "was this AI-written?" That's irrelevant. The real question is: is this output good enough to use? Nobody answers that at scale.

The Six Dimensions

⬡

Factual Accuracy

Claims are verifiable and grounded in reality, not hallucinated or stale.

◎

Relevance

Output actually addresses the stated brief, not a generic version of it.

▣

Depth & Completeness

Sufficient detail without padding. Substance, not word count.

◇

Clarity

Unambiguous, well-structured prose a real human can act on.

△

Originality

Non-generic, avoids the telltale boilerplate patterns of AI slop.

○

Fitness for Purpose

Appropriate for the stated use case, audience, and context.
